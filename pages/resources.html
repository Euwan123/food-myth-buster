🔬 Peer-Reviewed Studies
Systematic Review of MSG Safety in Human Consumption
PEER-REVIEWED
Tarasoff, L., & Kelly, M. F. (2000). Journal of Nutrition, 130(4), 1058S–1062S

Comprehensive review of clinical trials found no consistent evidence that MSG causes adverse reactions when consumed at normal dietary levels.

Effect of Sugar on Children's Behavior: A Meta-Analysis
META-ANALYSIS
Wolraich, M. L., et al. (1995). JAMA, 274(20), 1617–1621

Meta-analysis of 23 studies found that sugar does not affect the behavior or cognitive performance of children.

Organic Food Composition Compared with Conventional Food
PEER-REVIEWED
Barański, M., et al. (2014). British Journal of Nutrition, 112(5), 794–811

Systematic review found higher concentrations of antioxidants and lower pesticide residues in organic crops, but minimal nutritional differences overall.

FDA Safety Assessment of Monosodium Glutamate
OFFICIAL
U.S. Food & Drug Administration. (2023). FDA.gov

FDA classifies MSG as "generally recognized as safe" (GRAS). Extensive scientific review found no credible evidence of harm at normal consumption levels.

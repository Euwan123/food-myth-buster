🎯 Our Mission

Food myths spread fast — through social media, family advice, and cultural traditions. Food Myth Buster exists to slow that spread by providing a single, searchable place where anyone can verify a food claim and see exactly why it is true, false, or more complicated than it seems. Every verdict is tied to real research, and every page is built to be readable by anyone, not just scientists.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How are verdicts decided? ▼
Verdicts are assigned based on a review of peer-reviewed research, guidelines from health organizations (WHO, FDA, DOH), and scientific consensus. A verdict of TRUE means the claim is broadly supported by evidence. FALSE means the evidence contradicts it. PARTIAL means the truth is more nuanced — the claim is true under some conditions and false under others.
Can I submit a myth? ▼
Yes! Head to the Community page and fill in the submission form. You will need a free account. Submissions are reviewed before being added to the database. Please include a source link so the claim can be verified.
Is the AI analysis on search pages reliable? ▼
The AI analysis (powered by Anthropic’s Claude) is generated on demand for claims not yet in our database. It is useful as a starting point but should not be treated as medical or nutritional advice. Always verify with the primary sources linked on each myth page.
Is my data private? ▼
Accounts are managed securely via Supabase Auth. Your email is never displayed publicly. Submitted myths show your username on leaderboards only if you have submitted content. You can delete your bookmarks and activity from your profile page at any time.
Can I use this content for school or research? ▼
Yes, you are welcome to reference the site for educational purposes. Please cite the original research papers linked on each myth page rather than citing this site directly, since our verdicts are derived from those primary sources.
